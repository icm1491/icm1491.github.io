Italian Horror Films of the 1960s-70s

Introduction

Italian cinema has a long and diverse history, but this website was made to explore one genre of cinema: Horror! No matter the time or place, the genre of horror has always pushed the bounds of what is acceptable to show on the silver screen. Follow me (if you dare) as I explore how the Italian horror films of the 1960s and 1970s, and how they challenged societal expectations of the time!

The films that I watched were Profondo Rosso (Deep Red) by Dario Argento, La maschera del demonio (Black Sunday)by Mario Bava, and Suspira by Dario Argento. I wanted to explore the relationship between the expectation of a person in society and how it is exemplified/subverted in horror films. I have watched a lot of American horror films, with a focus on the slasher genre, so I know that horror can explore the relationship between people, their gender, and what society expects of that gender.
